# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want this project to be a respectful place for researchers, developers, students, and practitioners from different disciplines and levels of experience.

Expected behavior includes:

- discussing evidence and methods rather than attacking people;
- acknowledging uncertainty and disciplinary differences;
- giving constructive, specific feedback;
- respecting privacy, copyright, consent, and research ethics;
- welcoming questions and correcting mistakes transparently.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks;
- publishing private information without permission;
- sharing pirated papers, credentials, secrets, or sensitive research data;
- deliberate fabrication or misrepresentation of evidence;
- persistent spam or unrelated self-promotion.

## Scope

This code applies in repository Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and other project spaces.

## Reporting

For ordinary moderation concerns, contact the repository maintainer through the private contact method listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile. Do not publish sensitive reports in a public Issue.

Platform-level abuse may also be reported through GitHub's reporting tools.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior conflicts with this code. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused on community safety.
