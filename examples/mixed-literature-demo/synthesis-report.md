# Synthetic cross-paper synthesis

> Demonstration only. All studies, participants, values, quotations, and results in this folder are fictional.

## Scope

The demonstration contains three synthetic sources, three study units, eight page-verified claims, and five design-aware appraisal records. It is designed to test provenance rather than establish a real scientific conclusion.

## Verified findings

### Controlled experiment

The synthetic experiment reported progressively lower immediate recall means across the 0-, 30-, and 60-minute exposure groups. The paper reported an omnibus one-way ANOVA of `F(2,69)=5.84, p=0.004` (S0001, PDF page 2). The result is limited to a single simulated laboratory session and does not establish persistence or causation outside the synthetic setup (S0001, PDF page 3).

### Qualitative study

The synthetic interview study included 12 early-career researchers (S0002, PDF page 1). Its central theme was that page-linked audit trails were the main condition participants associated with trusting AI-generated summaries (S0002, PDF page 2). The analysts' involvement in both interview design and coding creates a reflexivity concern (S0002, PDF page 3).

### Computational benchmark

The synthetic transformer achieved macro-F1 values of 0.86 in-domain and 0.71 on a held-out domain (S0003, PDF page 2). Random document splitting increased macro-F1 by 0.06 compared with a domain-aware split, illustrating why split integrity must be checked (S0003, PDF page 2). External validity remains limited by the synthetic corpus and narrow domain coverage (S0003, PDF page 3).

## Cross-paper interpretation

The sources cannot be pooled because they address different questions with incompatible evidence types. Their shared methodological lesson is narrower: an auditable synthesis must preserve the study design, evidence location, comparison context, and appraisal boundary of each claim.

## Prohibited conclusion

These synthetic documents provide no evidence about actual blue-light effects, real researcher attitudes, or real classifier performance.
